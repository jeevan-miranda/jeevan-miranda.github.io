Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

What is neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the brain's ability to change and adapt due to experience.

    There are 2 types of neuroplasticity:
  • Functional plasticity is the brain's ability to move functions
  • Structural plasticity is the brain's ability to actually change its physical structure as a result of learning.

The benefits of understanding neuroplasticity

Some of the benefits of neuroplasticity are:

  • Adaptability: People who have a strong sense of neuroplasticity can adapt easier to new tasks and challenges.
  • Improved Problem Solving capabilities: Nueorplasticity supports cognitive flexibility allowing a person to approach problem solving in more creative approaches, helping them reach alternative solutions.
  • Improved Decision making: People with a flexible mindset can consider more diverse perspectives and use this information to make more informed decisions.
  • Greater openness to change: Neuroplasticity enables faster learning whihc in turn allows individuals to be more receptive and open to change.
  • Promotes Growth Mindset: Neuroplasticity aligns with the concept of a growth mindset, where individuals believe that abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work. Individuals with a growth mindset are more likely to embrace challenges, put in effort and seek improvement contiuously.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

There are few techniques you can use to engage and practice neuroplasticity:

  • Stimulating your brain in new ways can have positive changes on the brain. Stimulating the brain can take the form of learning a new language, or musical instrument, or practicing art.
  • Get plenty of rest: Sleep has been shown to have important effects on both physical and mental health. Practicing good sleep hygiene by having a consistent sleep schedule and creating an environment that contributes to good sleep can have a great impact on improving your sleep.
  • Practicing mindfulness: by being present in the moment and reflecting on the situation, your thoughts and feelings about a situation and your behaviours, you can adapt better, and help activate the brain plasticity

What is a Growth mindset?

Touching on it briefly before, growth mindset is the belief that our abilities can be improved through consistent effort and hardwork.

Without a growth mindset, we don't put in the required effort and this leads to a lack of growth and development. Having a 'Fixed' mindset causes us to shy away from challenges and growth opportunities as we don't want to look dumb infront of others, and this can lead to a lack of experiences that would benefit our ability to learn new skills and improve ourselves.

Developing a growth mindset helps us undertake a wider range of experiences that help us develop our skills.
