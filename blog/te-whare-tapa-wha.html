Te Whare Tapa Whā

My Te Whare Tapa Whā wellbeing plan

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model for developing an individual's health and wellbeing as a wharenui/meeting house with 4 walls.

    These walls represent:
  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whānau (family wellbeing)

In addition, there is another element whenua, which represents our connection to the land, and this forms the foundation of the wharenui.

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

Taha tinana is your physical wellbeing. It is about how your body grows, feels and moves, and how you care for it.

    My wellbeing commitments
  • Go to the gym 3 days a week.
  • Work on dieting and calorie counting

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

Taha hinengaro is your mind, heart, conscience, thoughts and feelings. It’s about how you feel, as well as how you communicate and think.

    My wellbeing commitments:
  • Go to the gym to improve endorphines and mental health
  • Practice reflection to develop mindfulness

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

Taha wairua explores your relationship with the environment, people and heritage in the past, present and future.

    My wellbeing commitments:
  • Practice self reflection

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

Taha whānau is about who makes you feel like you belong, who you care about and who you share your life with.

    My wellbeing commitments:
  • Take a more active role in engaging wiht my sibling and my friends to connect more and make sure Im having meaningful conversations with them to strengthen bonds

Whenua (land)

Whenua is the place where you stand. It is your connection to the land – a source of life, nourishment and wellbeing for everyone.

    My wellbeing commitments:
  • Go for more nature walk and do more walking trails around Auckland
  • Practice reflection and meditation outdoors so I have less distractions and can focus on only natural senses.
