Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence (EI) is the ability to manage both your own emotions and understand the emotions of people around you. There are five key elements to EI: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills.
People with strong emotional intelligence are able to understand their own emotions and the emotions of others and use that information to inform their own behaviour when approaching situations.

EQ is a measure of your emotional intelligence, assessing your ability to manage and express your emotions. It helps you identify your own feelings and the feelings of other and adjust your behaviour to approach situations in a calm and professional manner.
IQ is a measure of your capacity to learn, comprehend and apply knowledge in specific situations. It involves applying logic and verbal communication to solve complex issues.
Having a good EQ means you can emphatize with others and control your reactions, allowing you to manage relationships with clients and co-workers. A good IQ allows you to apply your knowledge in such a way that makes you an efficient and competent worker.
Both EQ and IQ are also developed in different ways. EQ is developed through reflections and practicing self-awareness to identify emotions and adjust behaviour accordingly. IQ is developed by seeking and practicing tangible knowledge and skills such as problem solving.

Both EQ and IQ are important to use when resolviong workplace conflicts. EQ allows you to understandf the other person's perspective and keep emoitonal responses in check, while IQ allows you to break down problems and apply logic to formulate solutions. Using EQ in tense scenarios is important, as it allows you to better manage the situation so that the other parties in the scenario are more receptive to working together towards formulating a solution to problems.
