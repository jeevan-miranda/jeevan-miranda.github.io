Identity Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now

In my role as a customer service rep for an accounting software company, I discovered an issue where the software was miscalculating an amount that populated in a specific financial report when investigating a case raised by an accountant. The issue meant that the report calculations were not in line with Internation Financial Reporting Standards.

I alerted the product engagement team who advised that the product team couldn't prioritise a fix as they were focused on delivering new features.

Ignoring the calculation issue would mean that our customer reports would not be correct, and may open them up to reporting or tax liabilities from regulatory agencies, so I decided to take the issue to a manager with previous accounting experience, and get them to liase directly with the Product owner to ensure the severity of the miscalculation is communicated and the fix received attention.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

One of my key strengths is that Im very detail oriented, so I try to always put alot of attention to ensuring I don't make mistakes in my work. I do this by having key frameworks developed for each task, and testing as I go to identify and resolve problems before moving on to later tasks.

My years in a customer experience roll have also helped me develop a unique social intelligence that helps me pick up cues from others to know how they are feeling and how they are motivated. This will help in team settings when working with my colleagues to complete tasks.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I've always been shy to ask questions and that has been a hinderence in my learning and understanding. I take great lengths to try being self-sufficient so I don't ask many questions, but it also means for issues I can't easily research or find resources for, I tend to fall behind in the pace that I learn and develop. It's something I am working to address.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I was tasked with mentoring a colleague to improve their quality of case management. I started the mentoring process by doing a shadowing session with them. During the session, I noticed they were nervous and appeared to be having mind blanks when investigating the issue of the ticket. I took a step back from the shadowing and pointed out my observations that they seemed to be extra nervous, which my colleague agreed.

We then had a discussion about how they learn the best, and their past experiences with learning. In doing so, we identified that he benefited from templates and written frameworks to guide his investigation, so we began working on written investigation guides for him to follow.
