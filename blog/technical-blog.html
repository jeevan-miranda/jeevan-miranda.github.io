Positioning

The property 'position: relative' sets the positioning of an element relative ot it's normal position in the flow of the webpage. Setting the top, bottom right or left properties will adjust the offset the element from it's normal position.

An analogy for relative positioning would be like moving a pawn chess peice two pieces forward on a chess board. When moving the piece, the squares moved is counted relative to where the pawn is currently located.

The property 'position: absolute' sets the element's position relative to the nearest ancestor (the positioned ancestor is the closes ancestor other than 'position: static')

Absolute positioned elements are removed from the normal flow of the webpage and can overlap with other elements.

An analogy for fixed positioning would be moving a chess piece to a specific position on the chess board, eg moving the bishop to B4. The position is determined by the coordinates on the board regardless of where other pieces are located.

'property: fixed' elements are positioned relative to the viewport, meaning that they will stay in the same place even if the page is scrolling. This is especially useful if you want to have an index displayed on a webpage that will continue to appear in the same position regardless of how far down a page a user scrolls.

An analogy that illustrates how fixed positioning works would be like glueing a chess piece to a square on the chessboard. It doesn't matter how the other pieces move around, the glued piece is fixed in place regardless of how others are able to be moved.
