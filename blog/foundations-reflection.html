Foundation Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I've learn't a few things about myself.

I'm quite risk averse, not just in my decisions but in my methodology and how I even code. I tend to spend lots of time researching before writing any code as opposed to trying something and testing. This is a hinderance in allowing me to grow and I need to get more comfortable with taking risks.

I'm a pretty good listener. It's always been one of my principles that when it comes to the work environment, you have to put ego aside and be open to listening, as it gives you more opportunities to learn and grow.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy and self-awareness enable you to understand people better. Once you understand your co-workers and internal and external customers, it becomes easier to reconize their wants and needs. This gives us the benefit of being able to better tailor the products we program to suit those needs.

As a programming environment is meant to be a collaborative environment where you work as a team, empathy and self awareness is also crucial in allowing us to de-escalate sensitive situations and resolve workplace conflicts so we can maintain that sense of collaboration and better serve our programming tasks.

Self awareness is also crucial for growth. Without constant self reflection and mindfulness, it becomes harder to learn and develop skills.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was skeptical about the practicality of some of the core learnings, however when I went through them and researched them for my blogs I realised how much of what I learnt was already behaviours I had put into practice, but never had a phrase to describe them before.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

With some of the core ideas such as neuroplasticity, I find it hard to think in terms of practical application. It sounds like it's a naturally occurring phenomenon that occurs when a person implements certain behaviours, however to do that you need to be mindful of how neuroplasticity works.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think learning the core behavioural developments helps develops a more well rounded programmer, who doesn't just have the technical knowledge to succeed in the role, but also the soft skills to apply themselves in a developer role.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I definitely don't think it's a waste of time. I've tried to be practical with my learnings and have tried to implement some behavioural changes to improve my mindfulness and slef-awareness at my workplace already and I feel that I've been able to improve my work and my relationship with my co-workers since starting this course.
